Recently, I am reading a book called *The Classic of Mountains and Seas*. In Wikipedia, it is described as a Chinese classic text and a compilation of mythic geography and myth. Versions of the text have existed since the 4th century BC, but the present form was not reached until the early Han dynasty. It is largely a fabulous geographical and cultural account of pre-Qin China as well as a collection of Chinese mythology. The book is divided into eighteen sections and describes over 550 mountains and 300 channels.
published: false

The book is very interesting because there are many strange animal and plant lives. Some of them are familiar, but most are imaginative. For example, Bifang is a bird with a human face; Yujiang has a human face and a bird's body; Qiongqi is similar to a tiger with wings. While reading, I can draw the image of them in my head — a rather interesting experience.
published: false

What's more, I also find things that differ from my initial understanding. For example, *Shuniao* is to the south of Kaiming, which has six heads whose names are Jiao, Fu, She, Zhui, Bao and Niao. At first, I thought it was another peculiar creature. However, after searching online, I found it is said to be equivalent to a guideboard in modern society, and the six heads represent six different tribes.
published: false

At last, I think the book is different from other ancient books. The language is plain and concise. But in another perspective, we can easily find the relations between different objects. In North America, some geographers have traced the book's descriptions and found that the positions of mountains and seas are remarkably similar to those in the book. Some people believe the book not only describes ancient China, but the whole world's natural environment.
published: false

Anyhow, the book attracts me a lot. I can find various imaginative things in it. Maybe I can buy some other relevant books and know more about this mysterious classic.
