Last winter vacation, I saw the movie *Wolf Totem*, adapted from the novel of the same name. The movie itself is impressive and calls for environmental protection. The people living on the grassland have their own beliefs, and the outsiders broke those beliefs — as a result, they believed they were punished by the gods. The grassland people revered nature through their beliefs, and their beliefs seemed to make them powerful. But in the end, it was also belief that made them too afraid to change their lives. A disaster happened on the grassland. So the question arises: will belief make us more powerful?

It is a difficult question to answer. Similar debates happen every day in the real world. For example, some people believe we should obey nature's laws and say no to GM technology, while others argue we must face food shortage and that GM technology is very important. It is hard to tell which is right. Belief is a mental representation of a sentient being's attitude toward the likelihood or truth of something. So I think we can regard belief as restraint — sometimes it produces a good effect as a baseline, but it can also become the bondage of exploration.

Under such circumstances, how to view belief may seem like a difficult choice — but it is also easy. If you are conservative, just do the reasonable things. If you are adventurous, try your best to explore the world. Both are power for the world; different people have different ways to contribute. This is the diversity of a population.

We can conclude that belief surely makes us more powerful, as different people have different opinions about it. Even in the movie, there is a brave person like the leading character who tries to cross the dogs and wolves and break the belief. So whether we choose to honor belief or break it, they are both roads to progress.
