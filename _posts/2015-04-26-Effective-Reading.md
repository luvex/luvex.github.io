This week, we talked about how to be an active and critical reader in class. I think it could be called effective reading, which is more and more important in modern society. So I'd like to gather the relevant material and reflect on my own reading habits.
published: false

In the textbook, six reading habits are offered by Harvard University. But I don't think they are suitable for everyone. So I will try to develop a new series of reading habits with the old ones.
published: false

**Firstly, previewing** is useful while reading a new book, however, just looking around is not enough. Maybe I could do more before reading in detail. Why not establish a knowledge graph before reading? As a coder, I can build a knowledge graph with JavaScript for data visualization. With the knowledge graph, I can find out the relations among many books and discover ones I haven't read yet. In this way, I will know more background and it may help me comprehend the book better.
published: false

**Secondly, annotating** is a good reading habit. Highlighting useful points makes it much easier to review the book after a period of time.
published: false

**Thirdly, outlining, summarizing and analyzing** are significant for understanding the essence of a book. Outlining gets us the architecture. Summarizing lets us retell the book. Analyzing lets us comprehend it in our own perspective.
published: false

**Fourthly, looking for repetitions and patterns** is also mentioned. But I don't think this is suitable for me — it is so complicated that too much time would be spent, and it may make reading boring.
published: false

**Fifthly, contextualizing** is another useful skill, especially in difficult parts of a book. However, it can lead to fragmented understanding if depended on too heavily.
published: false

**Finally, comparing and contrasting** — which is the most important in my view — is useful for any book. A knowledge graph can also be used here as an intuitive way to describe differences and analogies. For example, *Atlas Shrugged* and *The Fountainhead* are both written by *Ayn Rand*, and comparing the two books can reveal the author's change of thought over time.
published: false

Effective reading is also a key skill in modern society. With these habits, I am sure I can do better while reading.
