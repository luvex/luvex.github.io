At the end of today's class, we listened to a song called *Where Has All the Time Gone*. Our teacher suggested we think about passing time. For me, it is quite a good question. It has been 3 years since I graduated from high school, but to tell the truth, I didn't achieve most of the goals I set at first.
published: false

Maybe it is a good opportunity for me to reflect. My first goal is about my major — software engineering. My goal is to become a great coder, even a geek. So as a freshman, I told myself I must practice coding every day. However, I didn't do it very well. In the past 3 years, there were always all kinds of chores disturbing me so I seldom concentrated on coding. In my opinion, coding is an exciting job — I can create wonderful applications with a variety of programming languages and tools. So what is the problem? Maybe I always think too much and do too little. In the future, it is better to think less and concentrate on one project at a time, or I won't complete any of them.
published: false

The second goal is studying English well. When I was in high school, my listening and speaking skills were not very proficient. So I decided to get up early and practice them every day. But in most of the time, I didn't do it very well. Now my listening is better, but my oral English is still not good. I think the reason why I didn't make it is that the plan was too long and not clear, with no assessment of task fulfillment. So I won't develop a long plan for my English study — I'll just learn English in all possible fragments of time.
published: false

What's more, there are also many small goals during the past 3 years. Some were achieved, and some were not completed well. How time flies! Setting goals is always easy, but finishing them with persistent effort is difficult.
published: false

**Time is treasure — we must cherish it!**
